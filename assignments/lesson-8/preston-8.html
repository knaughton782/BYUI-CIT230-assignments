Enjoy the mild weekend. Big changes coming!

After a quick burst of precipitation early in the morning with the passing of a weak cool front, Thursday is shaping up to be partly sunny, dry and just a touch cooler than days past. Highs will stay above average, though, and remain warmer than normal through the weekend.

Sunday will be the warmest day of the extended period, with temperatures spiking into the mid 70s due to warm air being drawn into the region ahead of a potent cold front. That front – and the accompanying pool of cool air – will send temperatures plummeting early next week. Highs will drop 15°-20° from Sunday to Monday, and continue to decline through Halloween, which is shaping up to be cool with a slight chance of showers.
